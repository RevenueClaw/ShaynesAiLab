Reference List

Tools That Are Actually Useful

These tools have reputations for doing what they say. No sponsored picks, no personal endorsements — just a practical list for anyone curious about what's out there.

This isn't a "here's what I use" page. Different people need different things. Consider it a starting point for your own research.

ChatGPT Free

Free tier available. Plus at $20/mo for priority access, longer responses, and advanced models.

Widely used for drafting, brainstorming, analysis, and getting past writer's block. Known for being approachable with a conversational interface. Good entry point for anyone new to AI tools.

Common uses: Drafting, brainstorming, explaining topics, code snippets, creative writing.

Claude Free

Free tier available. Pro at $20/mo for higher usage limits and priority access.

Known for handling very long documents and nuanced analysis. The large context window lets it process full books or contracts in a single conversation. Often preferred for detailed analytical work.

Common uses: Analyzing long documents, contract review, detailed report writing, research synthesis.

Canva Free

Free tier available. Pro at $13/mo for brand kits, background removal, and premium templates.

Graphic design platform that makes creating visuals fast without needing design skills. AI features like Magic Write, Background Remover, and automatic resizing for different platforms save significant time.

Common uses: Social media graphics, presentations, thumbnails, brand materials, mockups.

Perplexity Free

Free tier available. Pro at $20/mo for unlimited searches and file uploads.

AI-powered search engine that provides answers with cited sources. Useful when you need verifiable information rather than generated content. Shows where each piece of information came from.

Common uses: Research with sources, fact-checking, staying current on topics, competitive analysis.

Notion Free

Free tier available. Plus at $10/mo for unlimited file uploads and guest access.

All-in-one workspace for notes, docs, wikis, and project management. Combines note-taking with databases, making it flexible enough to replace multiple tools. AI features include summarization and Q&A over your notes.

Common uses: Documentation, project tracking, knowledge bases, team wikis, personal notes.

Fireflies.ai Free

Free tier with limited credits. Paid plans start at $10/mo for more recording minutes and advanced features.

Meeting transcription and analysis tool. Records calls across platforms, transcribes them, and makes the content searchable. Helps capture action items and decisions without manual note-taking.

Common uses: Meeting recording, transcription, action item extraction, call notes, async review.

Cal.com Free

Free tier available. Paid plans start at $12/mo. Sign up →

Open source scheduling platform that eliminates the "what works for you?" email exchange. Share your availability link and let the other person pick a slot. Integrates with common calendar apps.

Common uses: Client meeting booking, team scheduling, event sign-ups, interview coordination.

Cloudflare Free

Free tier available. Paid plans for advanced security, performance, and Workers usage.

Content delivery network and web security platform. Free tier handles DNS, CDN, DDoS protection, and basic serverless functions through Workers. Powers many sites on the internet for good reason.

Common uses: Website hosting (Pages), DNS management, security, serverless functions, performance optimization.

Jotform Free

Free tier available. Paid plans start at $34/month for Bronze, more features at higher tiers.

Form builder with drag-and-drop interface, 10,000+ templates, and AI-powered form creation. Handles everything from simple contact forms to complex order forms, registration, and payment collection. Integrates with Make.com, Slack, HubSpot, and 100+ other tools.

Common uses: Lead capture forms, order forms, registration, surveys, questionnaires, payment collection.

Honest Recommendations

What I Actually Use

Nothing sponsored here. These are the tools I've used in my own business long enough to have a real opinion about.

I don't use every tool under the sun. These eight cover 90% of what I need every day. Some are free, some aren't — I'll tell you which is which.

A Note on Affiliate Links

Some of these links are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend tools I've actually used and found useful. If I wouldn't spend my own money on it, it's not on this page.

ChatGPT Free

$20/mo for Plus (optional)

My go-to for drafting, brainstorming, and explaining complex topics simply. The voice feature is surprisingly useful — I use it to talk ideas out while walking.

I use it for: Drafting emails that don't sound robotic, outlining blog posts, analysis, getting unstuck.

Claude Free

Free tier is plenty for most people

When I need to analyze long documents or contracts, Claude is my pick. The larger context window means it can handle way more text at once than most alternatives.

I use it for: Analyzing long PDFs, reviewing contracts, detailed report writing.

Canva Free

$13/mo for Pro (brand kits, background removal)

Thumbnails that used to take me an hour now take five minutes. The AI features (Magic Write, Background Remover, Resize) are genuinely useful, not gimmicks.

I use it for: Graphics, social posts, quick presentations, mockups.

Perplexity Free

$20/mo for Pro (more searches)

When I need to research something with real sources, Perplexity beats ChatGPT. It shows you where it got the information, which matters when you're fact-checking.

I use it for: Research with citations, fact-checking, staying current on industry regulations.

Notion Free

AI features included free

My brain lives here. Project docs, meeting notes, databases — it's the hub for everything that doesn't belong in email. The AI search is handy when I forget where I put something.

I use it for: Documentation, project tracking, team collaboration, personal notes.

Fireflies.ai Free

$10/mo for more recording minutes

I stopped taking meeting notes. Fireflies records calls, transcribes them, and I search the transcript later for action items. Saves me about 2 hours a week in note-taking alone.

I use it for: Recording calls, auto-transcription, finding action items without re-listening.

Calendly Free

Paid tiers for advanced event types

Eliminates the "what works for you?" email ping-pong. Setup once and send a link. Saves 2-3 emails per meeting — adds up fast when you're booking multiple calls a week.

I use it for: Client calls, team meetings, any time zone coordination.

Cloudflare Free

Paid plans for advanced features

I host all my sites through Cloudflare Pages and use them for DNS, CDN, and DDoS protection. If you have a static site, their free tier is more than enough.

I use it for: Website hosting, DNS management, security, workers/serverless functions.

The Honest Truth

I actually use more tools than these eight. I run custom OpenClaw automations, self-hosted AI models, and personal agents that took months to build. But that's not where anyone should start.

These eight tools cover the basics that just work. Master these first. Then decide if you ever need more.

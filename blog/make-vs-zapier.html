Make.com vs Zapier: Which Automation Platform Wins?

July 14, 2026

Quick Verdict

Make.com and Zapier both excel in different areas of automation workflows for small teams; Make.com offers visual workflow creation while Zapier provides more extensive integrations with over 3,000 apps. Ultimately, the choice depends on your specific needs—Choose based on ease-of-use vs integration breadth.

Overview

In this article, we compare two popular automation platforms: Make.com and Zapier. Both tools are designed to streamline workflows for small teams by connecting apps through automated triggers (events) that execute actions without manual intervention. However, they differ in their approach—Make.com focuses on visual workflow creation while Zapier offers a broader range of integrations.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Visual vs Code-Based Workflow Creation: Make.com's primary strength lies in its intuitive drag-and-drop interface, making it easy for non-developers to set up workflows visually. Zapier's advantage is the flexibility of using code (JavaScript) within Zaps when needed.

Integration Range and App Support: One significant difference between Make.com and Zapier lies in their integration capabilities with third-party apps. While both platforms support a wide range, Zapier boasts over 3,000 integrations compared to Make.com's more limited selection (as of the latest update). This makes Zapier suitable for teams requiring access to numerous niche applications.

Ease-of-Use and Learning Curve: Both tools are designed with user-friendliness in mind. However, those new to automation may find Make.com slightly easier due to its visual interface that simplifies the process of connecting apps without needing technical knowledge or coding skills. Zapier's learning curve can be steeper for beginners but offers more customization options as users become familiar.

Price Comparison

Make.com Pricing:

  • Free Plan: 1,000 workflows/month (limited app integrations)
  • Premium Plans start at $9.99 per month for unlimited apps and advanced features like custom code.

Zapier Pricing:

  • Starter Plan is free with up to five Zaps created, limited by the number of tasks performed (up to 10).
  • Professional Plans start at $49.99 per month for unlimited apps and advanced features like custom code.

The pricing structure reflects Zapier's broader range but also its higher cost compared with Make.com’s more affordable plans, especially considering the latter offers a generous free tier that many small teams can utilize effectively without incurring costs initially. However, as your team grows and requires advanced features like custom code or access to numerous apps beyond their initial offerings, both platforms' paid tiers become comparable.

Who Each Is For

Make.com: Ideal for small teams that prefer a visual approach without needing extensive coding knowledge. Best suited if your workflows involve fewer integrations or you are new to automation tools and want an easier learning curve initially.

Zapier: Better fit for more advanced users who need access to over 3,000 apps with the flexibility of using custom code within their Zaps when necessary (though this is not required). Suitable if your team’s workflows are complex or require a wide range of integrations beyond what Make.com offers.

5 Make.com Automations Every Ops Team Should Run

July 21, 2026

Why Make.com for Ops

If you read our Make.com vs Zapier vs n8n comparison, you know Make.com is our top pick for ops teams. What that article doesn't cover is the specific scenarios that save ops teams the most time.

Every scenario below runs on Make.com's free tier (1,000 operations/month). Each one saves 1-5 hours per week. Pick the ones that match your biggest pain points.

1. Email Triage Automation

What it does: Watches your shared team inbox, categorizes incoming emails by type, and routes them to the right person or channel.

Scenario structure:

  • Trigger: Gmail or Outlook — new email arrives in your shared inbox
  • Filter: Check subject line and sender for keywords (support@ → Support team, invoice@ → Accounting, meeting request → Calendly integration)
  • Actions:
    • Assign a label/category in Gmail (Support, Billing, Partnership, etc.)
    • Post a Slack message to the right channel with the email subject and preview
    • If high-priority (urgent keywords or from VIP sender), add a red urgency flag
    • Create a task in your project management tool if the email requires follow-up

Time saved: 3-5 hours/week for a shared inbox with 50+ emails/day.

Why Make.com wins here: Zapier's free tier limits you to 100 tasks/month — that's 2 days of email processing. Make.com's 1,000 ops/month covers 3+ weeks of the same workload.

Pro tip: Add a "Vacation responder detector" — if an email bounces back with "out of office" or "on vacation," archive it and add a reminder to follow up in 2 weeks.

2. Lead Routing & Qualification

What it does: Takes form submissions from your website and routes them based on lead quality and interest type.

Scenario structure:

  • Trigger: Jotform or HubSpot form — new submission
  • Router: Check the budget field, company size, and interest type
  • Branches:
    • High budget + relevant interest → Notify senior sales team immediately + add to priority CRM pipeline
    • Medium budget → Add to standard CRM pipeline + send standard intake confirmation
    • Low budget or vague inquiry → Add to nurture email list + send a "thanks for your interest" resource pack
  • Always runs: Log to Google Sheets, send confirmation email, post team notification

Time saved: 2-4 hours/week not manually reading and routing every form submission.

3. Invoice Follow-Up Reminder

What it does: Checks for overdue or upcoming invoices and sends automated follow-ups — no one has to chase payments.

Scenario structure:

  • Schedule: Runs daily at 9 AM (scheduled trigger)
  • Data source: Google Sheets or QuickBooks API — pull all invoices with status "sent" or "overdue"
  • Filter and route:
    • Due in 7 days → Send friendly reminder email to client ("Just a heads up, invoice #123 is due next week")
    • Overdue by 1-7 days → Send polite reminder + attach invoice PDF
    • Overdue by 8-14 days → Send firmer reminder + CC internal team member
    • Overdue by 15+ days → Post urgent Slack message to your team + flag in spreadsheet
  • Log: Record each reminder sent in a separate "Reminder History" sheet

Time saved: 1-2 hours/week not manually checking and following up on invoices. Also gets you paid faster.

4. Automated Weekly Reporting Digest

What it does: Collects data from all your ops tools every Monday morning and compiles a one-page summary for the team.

Scenario structure:

  • Schedule: Runs every Monday at 8 AM
  • Gather data:
    • Leads: How many new leads this week? (from CRM or Sheets)
    • Support: How many tickets resolved? Average response time? (from support tool)
    • Finances: How many invoices paid? Total revenue collected? (from accounting tool)
    • Tasks: How many tasks completed vs. open? (from project management tool)
  • Format: Use Make.com's text aggregator to build a clean summary
  • Deliver: Post to Slack channel + email to leadership team

Time saved: 1-2 hours/week not manually pulling reports from 4-5 different tools.

Pro tip: Add a "compared to last week" column. Make.com can store the previous week's data in a data store module and compare it. This turns a basic report into an actionable trend analysis.

5. Social Media Content Queue

What it does: Monitors your content sources (blog RSS, bookmarks, notes) and posts to your social channels on a schedule.

Scenario structure:

  • Trigger: Google Sheets or Airtable — new row added to "Social Content Queue"
  • Schedule: Posts at configured times (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri at 10 AM, Tue/Thu at 2 PM)
  • Actions:
    • Wait until the scheduled post time (use the Sleep module)
    • Post to LinkedIn, Twitter/X, or Facebook using Make.com's native modules
    • Mark the row as "Posted" in the queue spreadsheet
    • Log the post URL and engagement stats

Time saved: 1-3 hours/week not manually posting or scheduling each piece of content.

Why Make.com wins here: Buffer's free tier limits you to 3 posts per platform. Make.com lets you post unlimited times — you're only limited by the 1,000 ops/month cap, which covers 30+ posts.

Which One Should You Build First?

Automation Setup Time Weekly Time Saved Best For
Email Triage 45 min 3-5 hrs Teams with shared inbox chaos
Lead Routing 30 min 2-4 hrs Teams with inbound lead flow
Invoice Follow-Up 20 min 1-2 hrs Freelancers & agencies

Social Queue 25 min 1-3 hrs Content marketers

Start with email triage if you spend too much time in your inbox. Start with lead routing if you're missing follow-ups on form submissions. Either way, pick the one that hurts most — the others can wait until next sprint.

HubSpot vs Freshsales: Best Free CRM for Small Ops Teams in 2026

July 19, 2026

Disclosure: Some links in this article are affiliate links. If you sign up through them, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend tools I’ve tested and found genuinely useful for ops teams.

Quick Verdict

If you need a free CRM with solid contact management and basic sales automation, HubSpot’s free plan is the better choice for most small ops teams. Freshsales offers more advanced AI features and lead scoring in its free tier, but its free plan is more limited in storage and users. For teams under 10 people, Freshsales wins on AI; for teams up to 50, HubSpot’s free plan scales better.

Overview

HubSpot and Freshsales are two of the most popular free CRM options for small operations teams. Both offer free tiers that include contact management, deal tracking, and basic reporting. But they differ significantly in how they handle AI features, integrations, and usage limits.

HubSpot’s free plan is generous with users (unlimited) but limits storage (1,000 contacts) and advanced features. Freshsales’ free plan caps users at 10 but includes AI-powered lead scoring and email sequencing. For ops teams at 1-50 person companies, the choice often comes down to whether you need more users or better AI tools.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Contact Management

HubSpot allows unlimited users and up to 1,000 contacts on its free plan. You can track deals, activities, and notes. Freshsales also offers contact management but limits you to 10 users and 1,000 contacts. Both let you import contacts via CSV and integrate with email.

Winner: HubSpot (unlimited users is a big advantage for growing teams)

AI Features

Freshsales includes Freddy AI, which provides lead scoring, next-best-action suggestions, and email sentiment analysis on the free plan. HubSpot’s free plan has no AI features — you need to upgrade to at least the Starter plan ($20/month) for basic automation. Freshsales also offers AI-powered email sequencing (up to 5 sequences) on free.

Winner: Freshsales (AI lead scoring and sequencing are genuinely useful for small teams)

Integrations

HubSpot’s free plan integrates with over 1,000 apps via its App Marketplace, including Gmail, Outlook, and Slack. Freshsales integrates with about 200 apps natively, but you can use Make.com to connect to thousands more. Both support Zapier, but HubSpot has more native options out of the box.

Winner: HubSpot (more native integrations, especially for marketing and sales tools)

Reporting

HubSpot’s free plan includes basic dashboards for deals and activities. Freshsales offers more detailed reports on the free plan, including pipeline analytics and team performance. Both are limited compared to paid tiers, but Freshsales gives you more granular data.

Winner: Freshsales (better free reporting features)

Storage and Limits

HubSpot’s free plan includes 1 GB file storage and 1,000 contacts. Freshsales offers 1 GB storage and 1,000 contacts, but also limits users to 10. HubSpot has no user limit on free, making it better for larger teams.

Winner: HubSpot (unlimited users is a clear advantage)

Who Each Is For

HubSpot Free is best for:

  • Teams of 10-50 people who need a CRM with unlimited users
  • Ops teams that rely on many integrations (Gmail, Slack, project management tools)
  • Companies that plan to upgrade to paid HubSpot later (easy migration)
  • Teams that prioritize contact management and deal tracking over AI features

Freshsales Free is best for:

  • Smaller teams (under 10 users) that want AI lead scoring without paying
  • Ops teams that need email sequencing and sentiment analysis on a budget
  • Companies that prefer simpler, more focused CRM with less bloat
  • Teams that want better free reporting and pipeline analytics

Limitations to Know

Both free plans have significant limitations. HubSpot’s free plan lacks automation, AI, and advanced reporting — you’ll hit a wall quickly if you need to scale. Freshsales’ 10-user cap means you’ll outgrow it fast if your team grows. Neither free plan includes phone support (email only).

For ops teams that need more advanced workflow automation, consider pairing either CRM with Make.com to connect to other tools. For project management alongside CRM, a dedicated PM tool like Monday.com or Asana can work well. And if you need meeting transcription and note-taking, Fireflies.ai integrates with both CRMs.
